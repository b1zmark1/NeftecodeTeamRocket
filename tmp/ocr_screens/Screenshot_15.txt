Limited information has been published on the impact of
biodiesel on the durability of modem engines in cases with
high fuel dilution. Laboratory oil aging studies have reported
the effect of biodiesel dilution on engine oil: 10. 11 12
Work at Ford Research® has shown that fuel dilution resulted
in increased oil degradation, and biodiesel has a greater effect
on degradation than ULSD dilution. Bench tests with the
laboratory-aged oil with simulated fuel dilution showed wear
increases as oil age increases, but wear was not any worse
with biodiesel than with ULSD. Work by Watson et al 12
indicated that fuel dilution would be greater with biodiesel
than ULSD, and biodiesel dilution increased oil degradation
and copper corrosion. A bench test used to qualify oil as API
CI-4, High Temperature Corrosion Bench Test (HTCBT),
conducted with B20 showed increased levels of lead
corrosion! A laboratory study by Fang et al12 using

mixtures of base oil, zinc dithiodialkyl phosphate (ZDDP),
and fuel showed an increase in wear as the concentration of
aged biodiesel increased. The authors suggested partially
degraded biodiesel interacts with ZDDP, an anti-wear
additive, to hinder wear protection. Wear did not increase as
the amount of fresh biodiesel increased, indicating no
problematic interaction between ZDDP with fresh biodiesel.
