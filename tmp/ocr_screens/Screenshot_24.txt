Wear metals

Tron concentrations of the oil samples were determined to
provide an estimate of engine wear. A higher iron
concentration indicates more engine wear. The presence of
iron is also undesirable because it can act as a catalyst to
accelerate the rate of oil oxidation. Generally, the
concentration of iron in an oil sample increases as the wear of
engine components increases. However, in the case of this
fleet where large amounts of fuel continuously dilute the oil,
‘the concentration of iron could give a false indication of a
low amount of engine wear. Since the relative mass of iron is
of primary interest, wear metal (iron and lead) concentrations
were normalized to a level of zero percent fuel dilution using
the ASTM D3524 fuel dilution data.
