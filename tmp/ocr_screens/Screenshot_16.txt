‘November. B20 fuel was obtained from the Ann Arbor-Saline

collected at regular intervals throughout the duration of an oil
change interval. The volume of each oil sample was four
ounces, which was small enough to ensure that the engine did
not have to be topped off with any additional fresh oil. The

sump from overflowing from excessive fuel dilution.

interval of several minutes). This was due to several factors
including cooler ambient temperatures during the B20 trial,
lower energy density of B20, engine speed and load operation
shift due to energy density change, and a longer regeneration
event duration for the mileage backstop. Both the DPF
regeneration interval and the mass of fuel injected per
regeneration affect the fuel dilution rate. If the B20 trial was
run at the same ambient temperature as the ULSD trial, it is
likely that the mass of B20 injected per regeneration would
decrease, and thus the B20 dilution rate would also decrease.
