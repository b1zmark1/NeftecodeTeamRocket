Oil Age (miles)

Figure 14. TAN and TBN vs. mileage.

TBN depleted at a similar rate with B20 and ULSD dilution
Figure 14). This indicates that TBN depletion is primarily a
function of lubricant age, and not of biodiesel content. TBN
values with B20 dilution were ~1 mg KOH/ g oil less than
those with ULSD dilution across the entire oil interval. This,
initial difference in TBN value is unexpected considering that
both sets of vehicles had ~5% ULSD dilution at zero miles
and the vehicles running on B20 did not yet contain any
biodiesel. This may be explained by a difference in the TBN
of residual sump oil carried over at the initial oil changes.
After 12,000 miles, oi with B20 and ULSD dilution had
TBN values of ~1.3 and 2.3 mg KOH) g oil, respectively.
The TBN and TAN trend lines crossed over at ~8,600 miles
and ~9,200 miles for B20 and ULSD dilution, respectively.

Viscosity

Oil viscosity decreased with increasing mileage for both fuels
igure 15). The kinematic viscosity at 100 °C fell as low as
6.6 cSt in vehicles running on B20, and as low as 8.4 cSt in
vehicles running on ULSD. The oil generally fell out of its
SAE viscosity grade after only ~1,000 miles. The viscosity
grade dropped from SAE 15W-40 for fresh oil to xW-20 for
highly diluted oil. The lower oil viscosity of vehicles running
on B20 is explained by the higher fuel dilution levels as,
compared to vehicles running on ULSD (Figure 16). A rapid
increase in viscosity at high mileage, which is a sign of badly
degraded oil, was not observed with either type of fuel
dilution.
