The rate of lead corrosion is closely related to the
concentration of biodiesel oxidation products such as
peroxides and organic acids!3. 26 and also to the length of
time that lead-containing components are exposed to
biodiesel oxidation products. Since this fleet was
accumulating miles at a high rate, lead-containing parts were
‘only exposed to biodiesel and its oxidation products for a
relatively short period of time before changing the oil. It is
reasonable to believe that lead concentrations would be much
higher in the presence of biodiesel dilution if the oil intervals
were extended over longer time periods for the same amount
of miles.
