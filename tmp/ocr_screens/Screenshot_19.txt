Additionally, the speciation of FAMEs in  biodiesel-
containing oils changed only modestly and the location and
cis-trans isomerism of carbon-carbon double bonds in
unsaturated FAMEs did not change with age. The fatty acid
ester composition of the biodiesel fraction of oil samples
collected from Vehicle #143 is shown in Figure 8. Afier
1,499 miles, the oil contained 24 wt% biodiesel. The
biodiesel was made up of 29.5% methyl linoleate (C18:2),
33.9% methyl oleate (C18:1), 16.6% methyl palmitate
(C16:0), 10.1% methyl stearate (C18:0), 3.7% methyl
linolenate (C18:3), 3.0% methyl palmitoleate (C16:1), and
3.1% other acid esters, suggesting a biodiesel feedstock
containing about 40% soy oil and 60% lard (pig fat). As the
biodiesel fraction in the oil increased to 5.3 wit% after 3,994
miles, the proportion of fatty acid esters remained the same,
indicating the biodiesel feedstock did not change. At 5,398
miles, the proportion of fatty acid esters began to shift. The
C18:2 fraction increased from 29.5% to 38.0%, whereas the
fractions of C18:1, C16:0, and C18:0 decreased. The timing
of the shift corresponded to when the fuel station supplying
the B20 received a new batch of fuel. Based on the FAME
composition of the new fuel (in a fuel sample collected on
11/24/08), the biodiesel contained a different feedstock mix
(most closely matched by 20% soy oil, 20% lard, and 60%
com oil) than in the biodiesel used at the start of the B20 trial.
After this change in fuel, the proportion of FAMEs in the oil
gradually shifted through the end of the oil interval,
consistent with the accumulation/depletion of FAMEs from
the new fuel in the existing mixture from the prior fuel.
