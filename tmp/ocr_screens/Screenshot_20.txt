The relative proportions of C18:1 isomers remained
approximately constant with oil age Figure 10). Natural fats
and oils used as biodiesel feedstocks contain primarily the cis

isomers of carbon-carbon double bonds in unsaturated fatty
acids. Long-term exposure (hours) to high temperatures (e.g.,
220 °C) can result in conversion to a mixture of cis and trans
isomers, but this was not observed. It should also be noted
that the change in biodiesel feedstock did not have a
measureable effect on friction and wear performance, which
will be discussed later.

Oxidation

The IR-derived oxidation value of biodiesel-containing
samples cannot be interpreted the same way as samples that
do not contain biodiesel. This is because biodiesel is
composed of esters, which get counted as oxidation products
with the IR method. The oxidation value determined with IR
increases linearly with increasing concentration of fresh B20
in fresh oil (Figure 11). Fresh fuel from the second feedstock
